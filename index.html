Algebra Fundamentals

Quadratic Formula

$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$

The quadratic formula solves equations of the form ax² + bx + c = 0, where a ≠ 0.

Example:

For equation x² + 5x + 6 = 0:

$$x = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{25 - 24}}{2} = \frac{-5 \pm 1}{2}$$

Solutions: x = -2 or x = -3

Binomial Expansion

$$(a + b)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} a^{n-k} b^k$$

The binomial theorem describes the algebraic expansion of powers of a binomial.

Example:

For (x + y)²:

$$(x + y)^2 = x^2 + 2xy + y^2$$

Logarithm Properties

$$\log_a(xy) = \log_a(x) + \log_a(y)$$ $$\log_a\left(\frac{x}{y}\right) = \log_a(x) - \log_a(y)$$ $$\log_a(x^n) = n\log_a(x)$$

These fundamental logarithm properties are essential for solving exponential equations.

Geometry Essentials

Circle Area and Circumference

$$A = \pi r^2$$ $$C = 2\pi r$$

Area and circumference formulas for circles with radius r.

Pythagorean Theorem

$$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$

In a right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of squares of the other two sides.

Example:

If a = 3 and b = 4, then:

$$c = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = 5$$

Triangle Area

$$A = \frac{1}{2}bh$$ $$A = \sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}$$

Basic area formula using base and height, and Heron's formula using all three sides.

Calculus Fundamentals

Derivative Definition

$$f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}$$

The derivative represents the instantaneous rate of change of a function.

Power Rule

$$\frac{d}{dx}[x^n] = nx^{n-1}$$

The power rule for differentiation of polynomial functions.

Example:

$$\frac{d}{dx}[x^3] = 3x^2$$

Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

$$\int_a^b f'(x) dx = f(b) - f(a)$$

Links differentiation and integration as inverse operations.

Statistics & Probability

Mean and Standard Deviation

$$\mu = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i$$ $$\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i - \mu)^2}$$

Mean (μ) and standard deviation (σ) measure central tendency and spread.

Normal Distribution

$$f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}\right)^2}$$

The probability density function of the normal distribution.

Bayes' Theorem

$$P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)}$$

Describes the probability of an event based on prior knowledge of conditions.
