Quadratic Formula

$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$

For a quadratic equation of the form $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ where $a \neq 0$

Example: Solve $2x^2 + 5x - 3 = 0$
Here, $a = 2$, $b = 5$, $c = -3$
$x = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{25 + 24}}{4} = \frac{-5 \pm 7}{4}$
So $x = \frac{1}{2}$ or $x = -3$

Factoring Difference of Squares

$$a^2 - b^2 = (a + b)(a - b)$$

Useful for factoring expressions that are the difference of two perfect squares

‚à´Calculus Essentials

Derivative Rules

$$\frac{d}{dx}[x^n] = nx^{n-1}$$
$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^x] = e^x$$
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\ln(x)] = \frac{1}{x}$$

Integration Rules

$$\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C$$
$$\int e^x \, dx = e^x + C$$
$$\int \frac{1}{x} \, dx = \ln|x| + C$$
Note: Remember that $C$ represents the constant of integration in indefinite integrals.

üìèTrigonometry

Pythagorean Identity

$$\sin^2(\theta) + \cos^2(\theta) = 1$$

The fundamental trigonometric identity relating sine and cosine

Law of Cosines

$$c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab\cos(C)$$

Generalizes the Pythagorean theorem for any triangle

Common Angles

$\sin(30¬∞) = \frac{1}{2}$

$\sin(45¬∞) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$

$\sin(60¬∞) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$

Double Angle Formulas

$$\sin(2\theta) = 2\sin(\theta)\cos(\theta)$$
$$\cos(2\theta) = \cos^2(\theta) - \sin^2(\theta)$$

üìàStatistics & Probability

Mean and Standard Deviation

$$\bar{x} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i$$
$$\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i - \bar{x})^2}$$

Where $\bar{x}$ is the sample mean and $\sigma$ is the standard deviation

Normal Distribution

$$f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}\right)^2}$$

The probability density function for a normal distribution with mean $\mu$ and standard deviation $\sigma$
